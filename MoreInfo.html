Only 9% of all plastic waste in Canada is actually being recycled. This is due to the relatively high contamination rate of these materials. Whereby,The average contamination rate is 25%, meaning that 1 out of 4 items placed in a recycling bin cannot be recycled. This contamination leads to landfill overflow, facility risks, and increased costs for the government. This problem desperately needs a solution.

Effective and proper recycling actively combats a variety of enviromental issues that being anywhere from water pollution to climate change. In the case of climate change, uneffective recycling methods result in contaminated plastic being thrown into landfills - wherein due to constant sun exposure they release greenhouse such as ethylene or even methane whose potency is 25 times that of carbon dioxide.

Despite 72% of Canadian beverage containers being recycled, the average contamination rates reach are over 20% in cities like Edmonton, Toronto and Halifax. While food contamination does not affect the plastic recycling process, a small amount of food or liquid on a piece of paper can make a tonne of paper unrecyclable and as such drastically impacts the efficiency of the recycling process.
